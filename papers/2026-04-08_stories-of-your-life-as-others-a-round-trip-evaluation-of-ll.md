## Summary
This paper evaluates how well LLMs can encode real human personality profiles (from 290 participants) into first-person life story narratives, then uses separate LLMs to recover the original psychometric scores from those narratives alone. Recovery approaches human test-retest reliability (mean r=0.750, 85% of human ceiling) and is robust across 10 narrative-generating LLMs and 3 scoring LLMs from 6 providers. Content analysis confirms that personality conditioning produces behaviorally differentiated text, not just superficial trait-label alignment.

## Why It Matters
This work provides a rigorous round-trip methodology for validating LLM persona fidelity using real psychometric data, directly addressing whether personality conditioning produces meaningful individual-difference representations—a core question for researchers building and evaluating language model personas.

## Related Papers
### [Personality Traits in Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.00184) (2023)
This paper investigates whether LLMs exhibit measurable, stable personality traits as assessed by standard psychometric instruments (Big Five, Dark Triad), and explores methods for reliably shaping those traits via prompting. It provides foundational evidence that LLMs can simulate personality and raises questions about the validity of questionnaire-based evaluation.

### [Narrative identity and the storied self: A review of narrative approaches to personality] (2011)
Dan McAdams' work on narrative identity establishes that personal life stories are a primary vehicle through which personality is expressed and organized, providing the theoretical grounding for why life narratives are a valid medium for encoding and recovering psychometric personality profiles.
