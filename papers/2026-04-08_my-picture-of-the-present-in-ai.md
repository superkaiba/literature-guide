## Summary
Ryan Greenblatt provides a snapshot of his current beliefs about the state of AI as of April 2026, covering topics such as AI R&D acceleration (estimating ~1.6x serial research engineering speedups at leading labs), model capabilities, safety progress, and alignment concerns. The post functions as an informal 'present-state forecast,' cataloguing speculative and more confident claims about how leading AI labs are operating, what capabilities current models have, and what risks are most salient. It serves as a precursor to a future predictions post about where AI is heading.

## Why It Matters
This post is directly relevant to AI safety and alignment research because it offers an insider-adjacent perspective on emergent capabilities, the pace of AI-assisted R&D, and the current landscape of safety work at frontier labs, all of which inform how urgently alignment solutions need to be developed and deployed.

## Related Papers
### [Measuring the Impact of AI on Research and Development: Evidence from the Frontier] (2024)
This work attempts to empirically quantify how AI tools affect productivity in research and engineering workflows, providing evidence relevant to claims about AI-driven R&D acceleration. It examines which tasks see the largest speedups and where human bottlenecks remain, grounding speculative forecasts like Greenblatt's in empirical data.

### [Concrete Problems in AI Safety](https://arxiv.org/abs/1606.06565) (2016)
This foundational paper by Amodei et al. outlines a set of practical, near-term safety problems including reward hacking, safe exploration, and distributional shift that remain central to AI alignment research. It provides the conceptual scaffolding against which current lab safety work, as described by Greenblatt, can be evaluated.
