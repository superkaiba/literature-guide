## Summary
This post by Ryan Greenblatt is a 'present-state assessment' written as of April 2026, describing his best guesses about the current state of AI development rather than future predictions. The piece covers AI R&D acceleration, noting that serial research engineering speedups have reached approximately 1.6x at organizations like OpenAI and Anthropic due to AI-assisted tooling. It is structured as a collection of personal views and speculative claims about the current AI landscape, explicitly flagging more uncertain claims with hedging language ('I guess', 'I expect'). The post focuses on software acceleration, deployment integration, and the compounding effects of AI tools on AI research itself. It is framed as a precursor to a future predictions post, serving as a grounding document about the present baseline. The methodology is essentially expert elicitation from a single knowledgeable insider rather than empirical study.

## About the Authors
Ryan Greenblatt is a researcher at Redwood Research, known for work on AI control, sandbagging, and AI safety evaluations. He has published influential work on 'AI Control' (the framework for safely deploying potentially misaligned AI systems) and on eliciting latent knowledge. He is a prominent voice in the technical AI safety community, particularly around alignment and control under uncertainty.
